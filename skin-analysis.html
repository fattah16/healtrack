Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)
A common condition that makes skin red and itchy. It's common in children but can occur at any age. Eczema is long-lasting (chronic) and tends to flare periodically.

Common Symptoms:

  • Dry, sensitive skin
  • Intense itching
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Dark colored patches
  • Rough, leathery patches

Recommended Actions:

Moisturize regularly

Use fragrance-free moisturizers multiple times daily

Avoid triggers

Identify and avoid irritants like harsh soaps or allergens

Consult dermatologist

Schedule an appointment for proper diagnosis and treatment plan

Common Skin Conditions

Eczema
Chronic condition causing itchy, inflamed skin. Often appears in childhood.
Acne
Clogged hair follicles with oil and dead skin cells, causing pimples.
Psoriasis
Autoimmune condition causing rapid skin cell buildup, creating scales.
Sun Damage
UV exposure effects including sunburn, aging, and increased cancer risk.
Ringworm
Fungal infection causing circular, ring-shaped rash on the skin.
Rosacea
Chronic skin condition causing facial redness and visible blood vessels.
